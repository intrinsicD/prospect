# Novelty Taxonomy and Transformation Tests

## Operational definition

Literal novelty that is unrelated to all prior concepts is neither realistic nor verifiable. This skill instead distinguishes how an idea changes the known search space and whether it depends on newly generated evidence.

Novelty is always provisional and scoped to the search procedure, sources, terminology, and cutoff date.

## Classes

### N0 — Restatement

A reformulation, implementation improvement, scale-up, parameter change, or application with no distinct scientific claim.

### N1 — Direct recombination

A recognizable combination of known components, often expressible as known A + known B + target C. It can still be useful and publishable.

### N2 — Exploratory novelty

A previously unexplored point inside a known design space: parameter regime, interaction, domain application, or allowed component arrangement.

### N2-T — Unexplored transfer

A mature donor-field method is applied to a structurally compatible recipient problem while the recipient formulation remains mostly unchanged.

### N3 — Transformational novelty

The candidate alters the generative grammar of the field by changing at least one of:

- ontology or primitive objects;
- representation or state space;
- assumptions;
- objective or definition of success;
- admissible operators;
- symmetry or invariance requirements;
- unit of observation;
- causal direction;
- relationship between problem and solution.

### N3-T — Transformational mechanism transfer

A non-obvious structural correspondence imports a mature mechanism from another field and changes how the recipient field formulates questions or constructs solutions.

### N4 — Evidence-generating discovery

An experiment, proof search, simulation, anomaly search, or computation produces evidence absent from the initial corpus, and a resulting hypothesis materially depends on that new observation.

### N4-T — Evidence-driven transfer discovery

New recipient evidence reveals behavior matching a donor-field phenomenon; the transfer is induced by observation rather than selected only from textual analogy.

## Tests

### A-plus-B test

Can the candidate be adequately described as:

- known A + known B;
- known method on dataset/domain C;
- known method with component D replaced;
- larger, faster, learned, adaptive, differentiable, or multiscale A?

If yes, classify N1/N2 unless a distinct claim survives.

### Subtraction test

Remove every recognizable inherited component. State the remaining irreducible delta in one sentence. If nothing remains, the idea is not transformational.

### Grammar test

Does the idea change which solutions can be expressed or which questions can be asked? Selecting a new point in the old space is exploratory. Changing the space or its rules may be transformational.

### Prediction test

State a falsifiable prediction not already implied by the nearest prior methods. Without a distinct prediction, downgrade the candidate.

### Necessity test

Explain why the new primitive, state, invariant, or formulation is causally or mathematically necessary rather than decorative.

### Compression test

Explain the idea in two precise sentences. If it collapses into a familiar buzzword or standard method, lower the novelty score.

## Evidence required for high novelty

An N3/N3-T candidate should have:

- a precise changed primitive or rule;
- an irreducible delta;
- a new prediction;
- a reason native approaches cannot trivially express it;
- a falsification test.

An N4/N4-T candidate additionally requires an observation actually produced by an experiment or proof process, not merely a proposed future observation.
