## 1. Question and reason for the revision

MM-007 showed that physically matched R16/R32/R64 global and four-quadrant
translation did not recover a target-aware cross-fitted pixel ceiling on any of the
eight frozen videos. MM-008 asks whether R64 prediction requires smooth affine
deformation, global channel appearance, or their combination.

The first MM-008 design was stopped before real scoring because its synthetic controls
falsified two assumptions:

1. scalar coordinate descent could stop at an endpoint that required a coupled
   coefficient move; and
2. unrestricted gain/bias legitimately predicts an independent target's fitted mean,
   so persistence is not a valid source-use null for appearance.

The old rule that every excluded arm retain at least 90% of persistence error was also
invalid. In an additive combined target, either correct single family may remove a
large, real component while remaining incomplete. V2.2 uses exact finite-grid
selection, adds a matched target-only comparator to synthetic and real claims,
replaces universal negative margins with conditional dominance, and makes all
applicable endpoint evidence fail closed.

V2.2 remains a target-aware diagnostic ceiling. No fitted target parameter can enter the
production model.

## 2. Claim boundary

The eight videos and 453 half-second transitions are the already exposed,
outcome-informed MM-005--MM-007 panel. Videos are the eight support units. Parameters
are independently fit per transition against the true future target or a frozen wrong
target. Full fits are diagnostic; checkerboard-cross-fitted fits are primary.

A supported arm nominates a mechanism for a separately frozen causal
source-to-current estimator. It does not establish target isolation, population
prevalence, independent-video generalization, rollout quality, or end-to-end Prospect
capability.

The affine result is additionally bounded to the frozen 15,625-point grid and exact
float64 objective below. Every affine and combined context enumerates the complete
grid and selects its canonical global minimum; there is no continuous-parameter or
population optimum claim. For combined, "global" ranges only over the 2,809
admissible geometry states after applying the frozen clipped two-pass appearance
estimator at each state; it is not a global optimization theorem over arbitrary
appearance parameters. A clean real failure is therefore a failure of the tested
finite affine-grid family, not a theorem about all affine warps. Dense flow,
occlusion, disocclusion, nonlinear appearance, and new-content generation remain
outside scope.

MM-001 through MM-007 remain sealed and are never reclassified.

For native central coordinates,

```text

v = (x - 31.5) / 23.5
dy = ty + ayy*u + ayx*v
```

## 5. Target-only bias comparator

V2.2 retains `bias_only`, a target-marginal comparator. On exactly the same permitted fit
cells as appearance, set all gains to zero and fit one bias per channel. It uses the
same two passes and the same shared RGB macrocell retention rule:

1. fit each channel bias as the target mean over every permitted pixel;
2. rank permitted macrocells by the resulting RGB-averaged loss, retain the same
   `n-floor(n/4)` cells with stable macrocell-index ties, and refit each bias on the
   retained pixels.

Each raw pass-one and pass-two bias is independently clipped to the same `[-4,4]`
appearance bound. The final objective is the mean final RGB macrocell loss on the
same pass-one retained set; a third retention step is forbidden.

No target statistic crosses the checkerboard split. Full, true-target cross-fit,
near-wrong-target cross-fit, and far-wrong-target cross-fit bias predictions are
saved and scored against the true target exactly like their matched fitted arms.
Biases, retained macrocell IDs, predictions, SSE/count/MSE, and hashes are evidence,
not summary-only values.

This comparator answers whether an apparent gain preserved useful source content or
merely erased the source and predicted a fitted target mean. Persistence remains a
necessary physical comparator but is no longer sufficient for appearance or combined
support.
