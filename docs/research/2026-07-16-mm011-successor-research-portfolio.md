# Research portfolio: the first valid causal video assay after LCV-001

**Repository/domain:** Prospect; future-blind causal video prediction diagnostics  
**Literature cutoff:** 2026-07-16  
**Sources searched:** live repository, CVF Open Access, arXiv, primary registration
papers, official project repositories, NIST measurement guidance, and first-party
AIDE/AIDE2 materials. This was not an exhaustive patent, thesis, Scopus, Web of
Science, or private-code search.

## 1. Frontier map

| Stage | Established | Still unresolved |
|---|---|---|
| MM-007 | 477 authenticated R64 frames, 453 half-second causal triplets, four source-current fold normalizers; discrete translation and resolution alone did not help | Affine, appearance, combined, and genuinely future-blind prediction |
| MM-008 v2.2 | Exact-global affine, appearance, and combined estimators passed exposed-seed development controls | No formal or real-target result |
| MM-009 | Target-isolated protocol, predictors, baselines, scorer, decision ladder, sandbox, and synthetic panel were pre-real validated | The first post-marker operation failed in recursive MM-007 to MM-003 verification; no real row was constructed or scored |
| LCV-001 | Exact parent surface, lexical runtime, one-thread OpenBLAS, canary, controls, and cleanup passed two independent audits | No fitting, prediction, or scoring |

The sparse frontier cell is the first completed real causal assay combining the
LCV-authenticated parent, the v2.2 estimators, and MM-009's future-blind scientific
grammar.

## 2. Functional problem signature

Two observed past states enter. A hidden transformation must be inferred and applied
once more without access to the next state. The assay must distinguish transported
content from innovation, occlusion, appearance change, static-background dominance,
and interpolation artifacts. It has eight autocorrelated support units, passive
observations, a 0.5-second cadence, and a point-error objective. A valid result needs
both causal improvement and controls that reject current-only bias, non-directional
similarity, shuffled history, reversed motion, and target leakage.

## 3. Fixation anti-library

The default but currently unhelpful answers are a larger video model, generic optical
flow, an encoder swap, threshold relaxation, best-arm post-selection, target-aware
search, or AIDE-style iteration against the same eight futures. Each changes the
question before the first valid execution and makes a negative result harder to
interpret.

## 4. Productive recombinations

### Candidate 1 — LCV-backed causal operator sufficiency

**Central claim:** At least one unchanged affine, appearance, or combined operator
fitted only on `previous -> current` satisfies the complete future gate when applied
once to `current`.  
**Novelty class:** N1; the value is new evidence, not algorithm novelty.  
**Known foundation:** Parametric registration, warping, and MM-008/MM-009.  
**Irreducible delta:** First real target-isolated observation under a verified
lineage/runtime.  
**Why this is not merely A + B:** It is deliberately A+B scientifically; its research
value is closing a missing experimental cell without confounding changes.  
**Changed grammar or transfer mechanism:** None; preserve the frozen causal grammar.  
**New prediction:** One exact family either crosses the full joint gate or the finite
operator family is cleanly eliminated on this panel.  
**Cheapest killing test:** The unchanged 453-row, eight-video one-shot assay. Null
hypothesis: no family passes its complete gate.  
**Prior-art threats:** Classical affine registration, future-flow extrapolation, and
parametric video prediction.  
**Novelty confidence:** 0–5%; cutoff and sources above.  
**Scientific value:** Highest immediate causal information.  
**Publishable if successful/partial/failed:** A bounded mechanism result, a localized
family/control boundary, or a rigorous elimination of the cheapest operator class.

### Candidate 2 — History-only selective operator

**Central claim:** Source-only reconstruction, shuffle separation, and conditioning
identify a useful coverage subset on which forecasts beat persistence.  
**Novelty class:** N2.  
**Known foundation:** Selective prediction and flow consistency.  
**Irreducible delta:** A frozen target-free transportability certificate.  
**Why this is not merely A + B:** The certificate must predict causal transport rather
than merely alignment quality.  
**Changed grammar or transfer mechanism:** Point prediction becomes prediction plus
abstention.  
**New prediction:** Risk decreases monotonically as source-only certificate quality
increases.  
**Cheapest killing test:** Train-fold or synthetic calibration, held-out-video
risk/coverage; abandon below 25% useful coverage or random-selector parity.  
**Prior-art threats:** Forward/backward optical-flow confidence.  
**Novelty confidence:** 10–30%.  
**Scientific value:** Separates operator insufficiency from unobservable validity.  
**Publishable if successful/partial/failed:** A selector, a calibrated limitation, or
evidence that historical fit cannot certify forecasting.

### Candidate 3 — Two-interval operator acceleration

**Central claim:** Change between two prior transition operators predicts the next
operator better than repeating the latest one.  
**Novelty class:** N2.  
**Known foundation:** Constant-acceleration motion and dynamic system identification.  
**Irreducible delta:** Make the inferred operator dynamic instead of fixed.  
**Why this is not merely A + B:** It predicts an operator derivative under the same
causal controls.  
**Changed grammar or transfer mechanism:** State includes an operator trajectory.  
**New prediction:** Acceleration helps only where repeated-operator residuals have a
consistent sign/structure.  
**Cheapest killing test:** Add one past frame and compare against MM-011 under the same
nulls; abandon without preregistered per-video gain.  
**Prior-art threats:** Constant-velocity flow and future-flow prediction.  
**Novelty confidence:** 10–25%.  
**Scientific value:** Directly tests operator drift.  
**Publishable if successful/partial/failed:** Dynamic evidence, a regime map, or
rejection of missing acceleration.

### Candidate 4 — Transport plus tiny residual predictor

**Central claim:** A held-out-video `3x3` residual model predicts innovation remaining
after global transport.  
**Novelty class:** N1/N2.  
**Known foundation:** Propagation-plus-generation video prediction.  
**Irreducible delta:** Strict future-blind separation of transport and innovation.  
**Why this is not merely A + B:** Only useful if transport residuals prove stable
across videos.  
**Changed grammar or transfer mechanism:** Decompose prediction into transport and
source/sink innovation.  
**New prediction:** Residual gain concentrates at disocclusion or appearance-change
regions.  
**Cheapest killing test:** Held-out-video residual gain over transport and residual-only
baselines; abandon if it learns current-frame bias.  
**Prior-art threats:** Disentangled propagation/generation models.  
**Novelty confidence:** 5–15%.  
**Scientific value:** Explains what global transport cannot express.  
**Publishable if successful/partial/failed:** A causal decomposition, localized error
taxonomy, or evidence that residuals are not forecastable at this scale.

## 5. Exploratory candidates

### Candidate 5 — Subpixel phase-shift arm

**Central claim:** Continuous Fourier phase correlation recovers motion missed by the
four-pixel translation grid.  
**Novelty class:** N2-T from signal processing.  
**Known foundation:** Subpixel image registration.  
**Irreducible delta:** A cheap continuous global-shift comparator under the same causal
gate.  
**Why this is not merely A + B:** It tests whether quantization, not motion class, caused
the earlier null.  
**Changed grammar or transfer mechanism:** Continuous rather than finite-grid shift.  
**New prediction:** Gain appears on active videos with stable phase peaks.  
**Cheapest killing test:** Compare with persistence, discrete translation, shuffled
targets, and phase randomization; abandon at 0/8 support.  
**Prior-art threats:** Phase correlation is classical.  
**Novelty confidence:** 5–20%.  
**Scientific value:** Cheapest continuous-registration escape hatch.  
**Publishable if successful/partial/failed:** Quantization diagnosis, regime boundary,
or closure of global translation.

### Candidate 6 — Causal cadence phase diagram

**Central claim:** Operator transportability has a cadence breakpoint rather than a
smooth decay.  
**Novelty class:** N2, potentially N4 only after a new observation.  
**Known foundation:** Horizon diagnostics and temporal sampling.  
**Irreducible delta:** Measure a mechanism boundary across matched causal cadences.  
**Why this is not merely A + B:** The outcome is a phase map, not just another horizon.  
**Changed grammar or transfer mechanism:** Cadence is an intervention variable.  
**New prediction:** Support changes discontinuously between 0.5 and 1.0 seconds.  
**Cheapest killing test:** Two matched cadence cells; abandon if support/control
patterns are equivalent within frozen tolerance.  
**Prior-art threats:** Multi-horizon video prediction.  
**Novelty confidence:** 15–35%.  
**Scientific value:** Maps an identifiability/transport boundary.  
**Publishable if successful/partial/failed:** Phase transition, graded boundary, or
cadence-invariant impossibility region.

### Candidate 7 — Operator Gauge R&R

**Central claim:** Variation across checkerboards, macrocells, resolutions, and small
source perturbations separates estimator repeatability from scene reproducibility and
predicts forecast failure.  
**Novelty class:** N2-T measurement transfer from metrology.  
**Known foundation:** Gauge repeatability and reproducibility analysis.  
**Irreducible delta:** Treat operator fitting as a measurement process.  
**Why this is not merely A + B:** It imports failure measurement, not another predictor.  
**Changed grammar or transfer mechanism:** A fit carries a variance budget.  
**New prediction:** High pseudo-replicate variance predicts null/reversal failure.  
**Cheapest killing test:** Freeze admissible perturbations and compare their variance
with held-out future error; abandon if uncorrelated.  
**Prior-art threats:** Bootstrap uncertainty and flow-confidence diagnostics.  
**Novelty confidence:** 30–55% for the correspondence, not the donor method.  
**Scientific value:** Strong informative-negative-result protocol.  
**Publishable if successful/partial/failed:** A measurement diagnostic, a limited
variance decomposition, or evidence that fit stability is non-predictive.

### Candidate 8 — Causal rate-distortion accounting

**Central claim:** Families should be ranked by future residual reduction per fixed
parameter-description cost.  
**Novelty class:** N2-T from video compression.  
**Known foundation:** Motion compensation and rate-distortion.  
**Irreducible delta:** A causal innovation budget for diagnostic operators.  
**Why this is not merely A + B:** Complexity is charged before target evaluation.  
**Changed grammar or transfer mechanism:** Success balances predictive residual and
description length.  
**New prediction:** Stable simple operators dominate apparent high-capacity gains per
description bit.  
**Cheapest killing test:** Preregister code lengths; abandon if ranking duplicates raw
MSE or depends on arbitrary constants.  
**Prior-art threats:** Parametric motion-compensated coding.  
**Novelty confidence:** 10–25%.  
**Scientific value:** Exposes unstable complexity.  
**Publishable if successful/partial/failed:** A causal accounting rule, sensitivity
analysis, or rejection of description cost as explanatory.

## 6. Transformational candidates

### Candidate 9 — Real-valued operator syndrome

**Central claim:** Redundant checkerboard, reversal, composition, and shuffle
constraints define a real-valued syndrome that certifies or rejects a forecast before
the target is visible.  
**Novelty class:** candidate N3-T from coding theory.  
**Known foundation:** Error-detecting syndromes and consistency checks.  
**Irreducible delta:** Forecast validity becomes membership in a constrained syndrome
region.  
**Why this is not merely A + B:** The certificate, not the predictor, is the primitive.  
**Changed grammar or transfer mechanism:** `forecast -> forecast + certificate + abstention`.  
**New prediction:** Syndrome magnitude orders held-out risk better than any single
native diagnostic.  
**Cheapest killing test:** Synthetic corruptions then held-out risk/coverage; abandon
without monotone ordering.  
**Prior-art threats:** Flow consistency and selective prediction.  
**Novelty confidence:** 20–45%.  
**Scientific value:** Could make causal validity observable without future access.  
**Publishable if successful/partial/failed:** Certificate method, partial redundancy
map, or a negative certificate-sufficiency result.

### Candidate 10 — Near-optimal operator reachable set

**Central claim:** Propagating all finite-grid operators within a frozen history-loss
tolerance yields a calibrated, sharp future set when a point estimate is unidentified.  
**Novelty class:** candidate N3-T from formal methods.  
**Known foundation:** Abstract interpretation, reachable sets, and conformal sets.  
**Irreducible delta:** The prediction object is a set of reachable pixels/operators.  
**Why this is not merely A + B:** Success becomes coverage plus sharpness rather than
point MSE.  
**Changed grammar or transfer mechanism:** Replace point construction with a bounded
set characterization.  
**New prediction:** Ambiguous source histories yield wider but calibrated sets.  
**Cheapest killing test:** Reuse the finite grid; abandon if miscalibrated or no sharper
than the trivial pixel range.  
**Prior-art threats:** Conformal operator learning and generic reachability.  
**Novelty confidence:** 25–50%.  
**Scientific value:** Directly represents two-frame ambiguity.  
**Publishable if successful/partial/failed:** A set predictor, ambiguity bound, or proof
that this operator grammar is uninformative.

### Candidate 11 — Operator-semigroup defect as state

**Central claim:** A transition-operator trajectory and its composition defect are a
better predictive state than the last point operator.  
**Novelty class:** candidate N3.  
**Known foundation:** Koopman/DMD and semigroup dynamics.  
**Irreducible delta:** Make failure of repeated application an explicit state variable.  
**Why this is not merely A + B:** The defect changes what the assay treats as state.  
**Changed grammar or transfer mechanism:** Predict operator evolution and composition
validity, not pixels alone.  
**New prediction:** Source-only defect predicts MM-011 residuals and identifies where a
correction helps.  
**Cheapest killing test:** Correlate frozen defect with future error/null outcomes;
abandon if unrelated.  
**Prior-art threats:** Koopman operator learning and transition-consistency losses.  
**Novelty confidence:** 15–40%.  
**Scientific value:** Strongest theory direction.  
**Publishable if successful/partial/failed:** New state formulation, empirical defect
map, or rejection of semigroup failure as the limiter.

## 7. Cross-domain transfers

| Transfer | State/observation/operator | Preserved mechanism | Broken correspondences | Required invention / prediction | Native competitor | Adoption barrier |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| System identification | transform / pixels / fit | unexcited observations cannot identify dynamics | passive pixels; identifiability does not imply persistence; nuisance texture | source-only excitation statistic; low excitation cannot support causal gain | history-fit diagnostics | only eight videos |
| Metrology | measurand / repeated gauge / variance budget | repeatability differs from reproducibility and bias | pseudo-replicates are not physical repeats; scenes are heterogeneous; perturbations may change the measurand | frozen pseudo-replicates; high variance predicts failure | bootstrap confidence | defining valid repeats |
| Coding theory | valid codeword / parity checks / syndrome | structured redundancy detects corruption without target content | checks are real-valued; valid scenes need not have zero syndrome; errors are not discrete | calibrated syndrome region; monotone risk/coverage | flow consistency | reliable calibration |
| Formal methods | state set / abstract transformer / reachable set | overapproximation can certify inclusion | empirical history tolerance is not a physical bound; image noise is unbounded; pixel sets may explode | finite-grid reachable set; calibrated sharpness | point forecast + conformal residual | set width |
| Unbalanced transport | radiance / warp / source-sink | conservation failure is modeled explicitly | RGB is signed after normalization; brightness is not mass; occlusion is only partly observable | channel gauge and source/sink observable; error localizes with innovation | residual predictor | physically valid mapping |
| Video compression | reference / motion / residual / code cost | compact stable transport leaves a small innovation payload | no real bitstream; future motion unknown; code length can be arbitrary | frozen description cost; simple families win per bit | raw-MSE ranking | preregistering fair costs |

The first four transfers pass terminology removal and preserve a causal mechanism.
Their mismatches are scientifically productive, not evidence of adoption. None is
promoted ahead of MM-011.

## 8. New-evidence discovery programs

### Program A — Off-grid sensitivity map

**Search space:** 1–3 pixel translation, half-step affine coefficients, mild drift,
low texture, noise, and occlusion on generated data.  
**Observable:** history reconstruction, parameter recovery, forecast margin, null and
directional predicates.  
**Conventional expectation:** exact-grid success degrades smoothly.  
**Surprising signature:** sharp quantization collapse or a stable off-grid margin.  
**Promotion rule:** reproducible failure promotes continuous registration; stable
margin permits unchanged MM-011 freeze.  
**Falsification and controls:** known generators, seed freeze, identity/reversal/null
cases, exact-array replay, and no real future access.  
**Reproduction package:** pinned seeds/config/runtime and machine-readable evidence.

### Program B — Cadence and defect map

**Search space:** matched 0.5/1.0-second histories and operator-composition defects.  
**Observable:** family support, source-only defect, future residual, and null health.  
**Conventional expectation:** gradual horizon degradation.  
**Surprising signature:** cadence breakpoint or defect-conditioned regime split.  
**Promotion rule:** replicated held-out-video split becomes a new hypothesis lineage.  
**Falsification and controls:** same videos/folds/baselines, direction reversal, shuffle,
and preregistered multiple-comparison handling.  
**Reproduction package:** exact frame identities, seeds, tolerances, and result digest.

Neither program is N4 yet; that requires a produced observation.

## 10. Recommended first experiment

Build **MM-011 — LCV-backed causal deformation/appearance prediction** under a new
identity and empty canonical root. Preserve MM-009's estimator semantics, 453 rows,
baselines, thresholds, decision precedence, prediction freeze, target isolation, and
synthetic controls. Mechanically rebind every schema/hash/result identity; replace the
recursive historical verifier and resolved-interpreter supervisor with a copied exact
LCV receipt, LCV-owned parent semantics, and lexical/terminal runtime revalidation.

Null hypothesis: no exact family satisfies the complete frozen gate. The decisive
positive observation is at least one family passing joint history/future support while
all shuffle, reverse, derangement, bias, isolation, and runtime controls stay clean.
Any custody failure is invalid rather than scientific. A clean failure abandons only
this exact finite-grid, half-second, global affine/appearance/combined family.

Run the off-grid synthetic program before freeze. Do not adapt on real futures. AIDE2
waits until several cheap development tasks and a physically withheld private panel
exist; otherwise the research harness would optimize the judge rather than test the
mechanism.

## 11. Audit limitations

- The eight videos informed earlier diagnostics; this is exploratory mechanism
  selection, not independent confirmation.
- Transitions within a video are autocorrelated; videos, not rows, are support units.
- Whole-video folds isolate normalizers; row-local operators do not establish
  cross-video predictor generalization.
- Pixel MSE can reward smoothing, so gradient/visual diagnostics remain descriptive
  guardrails.
- Global operators cannot express independent object motion, occlusion, disocclusion,
  or new content.
- Novelty confidence is provisional under the stated search; AIDE2 details beyond its
  first-party report were not treated as established technical evidence.
